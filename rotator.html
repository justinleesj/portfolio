This project involved the design of a hypocycloid drive mechanism modeled in PTC Creo, with all parts engineered for either additive manufacturing or procurement from McMaster-Carr. The system relies on interlinked triangular arms fitted with ball bearings to transmit rotary motion smoothly and continuously. Unlike conventional gear trains, this configuration achieves rolling contact between elements, reducing backlash while maintaining a compact footprint. The mechanism is supported by a custom frame and powered by a motor mount at the rear, allowing for straightforward integration into larger electromechanical assemblies.

The design emphasized manufacturability and accessibility: structural components were dimensioned for 3D printing with standard tolerances, while shafts, fasteners, and bearings were specified directly from McMaster-Carr to ensure reliable sourcing. This approach made it possible to prototype a complex transmission system with minimal cost and lead time.
